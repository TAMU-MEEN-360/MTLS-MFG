Static Loading + Flaw Example

Example 7-1 — Design of a Nondestructive Test

Problem
A large steel plate used in a nuclear reactor has a plane strain fracture toughness of \(K_{IC} = 80{,}000\;psi\sqrt{in}\) and experiences a service stress of \(45{,}000\;psi\).
Design a testing or inspection procedure capable of detecting a crack at the edge of the plate before the crack is likely to grow at a catastrophic rate.
Step 1 — Fracture Condition
Failure occurs when the stress intensity factor equals the fracture toughness. For an edge crack:
\(K_I = f\sigma\sqrt{\pi a}\)
For a single-edge crack, the geometry factor is approximately:
\(f = 1.12\)
Step 2 — Solve for the Critical Crack Size
Substitute the known values into the fracture toughness equation.
\(80{,}000 = (1.12)(45{,}000)\sqrt{\pi a}\)
Solving for the crack length \(a\):
\(a = 0.8\;in.\)
Design Requirement
Critical crack size: \(a_c = 0.8\;in.\) Inspection methods must detect cracks **smaller than 0.8 in.** before catastrophic fracture can occur.
Engineering Interpretation
A crack of this size should be relatively easy to detect. In many cases, cracks approaching 0.8 inches can be observed visually. Several nondestructive testing methods are capable of detecting much smaller cracks, including:
  • Dye penetrant inspection
  • Magnetic particle inspection
  • Eddy current inspection
If inspections are performed regularly and crack growth is slow, a flaw should be discovered long before reaching the critical crack size that would cause catastrophic failure.
