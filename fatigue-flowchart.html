Failure
Static loading + flaw
\( K = f\sigma\sqrt{\pi a} \)
Failure if \(K \ge K_{IC}\)
  • \(K\): stress intensity factor
  • \(\sigma\): applied stress
  • \(a\): crack size parameter
  • \(f\): geometry factor
  • \(K_{IC}\): fracture toughness
Note on crack length:
Center crack total length = \(2a\).
Edge crack length = \(a\).
Examples:
• Pressure vessel weld crack
• Brittle plate with notch
• Ceramic component under steady load
• Structural bracket with sharp flaw
• Glass panel with surface crack

Single/slow load where a crack controls immediate fracture.
Fatigue (no flaw)
Use S–N curve / endurance limit
  • \(\sigma_a\): stress amplitude
  • \(\sigma_m\): mean stress
  • \(R\): stress ratio
  • \(N\): cycles to failure
Examples:
• Rotating shaft
• Spring under repeated loading
• Bolt under vibration
• Gear tooth root fatigue
• Bike frame under road cycles

You calculate: Fatigue life or allowable stress.
Cyclic loading with no dominant initial crack.
Fatigue + flaw
\( \dfrac{da}{dN} = C(\Delta K)^n \)
\( \Delta K = f(\sigma_{max}-\sigma_{min})\sqrt{\pi a} \)
  • \(a\): crack size
  • \(\Delta K\): stress intensity range
  • \(C,n\): material constants
  • \(\sigma_{max}, \sigma_{min}\): cycle limits
Center crack uses total \(2a\); edge crack uses \(a\).
Examples:
• Aircraft fuselage crack growth
• Pipeline under pressure cycles
• Bridge weld crack under traffic
• Turbine blade defect
• Structural plate edge crack under cyclic tension
