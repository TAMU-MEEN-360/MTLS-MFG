Failure
Static loading + flaw
\( K = f\,\sigma\,\sqrt{\pi a} \)
Failure if \(K \ge K_{IC}\)
Variables
  • \(K\): stress intensity factor (Mode I here)
  • \(\sigma\): applied (nominal) stress
  • \(a\): crack size parameter
  • \(f\): geometry factor (also written \(Y\))
  • \(K_{IC}\): plane-strain fracture toughness
\(a\) vs \(2a\): Center crack is often labeled total length \(2a\) (so \(a\) is half-length). Edge cracks are usually just labeled \(a\) from the edge inward. Keep your \(a\) definition consistent with \(f\).
Examples
• Pressure vessel with known weld defect under a one-time proof load
• Brittle plate with an edge notch pulled in tension once
• Glass/ceramic part with a surface crack under steady load
• Structural bracket with a sharp crack loaded once near max service load
When to use
One-time/slow loading where a flaw controls immediate failure.
Fatigue (no flaw)
S–N / endurance approach
Use stress amplitude \(\sigma_a\) vs cycles to failure \(N\)
Common variables
  • \(\sigma_{max}, \sigma_{min}\): max/min stress in a cycle
  • \(\sigma_a = (\sigma_{max}-\sigma_{min})/2\): stress amplitude
  • \(\sigma_m = (\sigma_{max}+\sigma_{min})/2\): mean stress
  • \(R = \sigma_{min}/\sigma_{max}\): stress ratio
  • \(N\): cycles to failure
Examples
• Rotating shaft in bending
• Springs under repeated loading
• Bolts experiencing vibration cycles
• Gear tooth root bending under repeated meshing cycles
When to use
Cyclic loading when you assume no dominant pre-existing crack.
Fatigue + flaw
\( \dfrac{da}{dN} = C(\Delta K)^n \)
\( \Delta K = f(\sigma_{max}-\sigma_{min})\sqrt{\pi a} \)
Variables
  • \(a\): crack size parameter
  • \(N\): number of cycles
  • \(\frac{da}{dN}\): crack growth per cycle
  • \(\Delta K\): range of stress intensity in a cycle
  • \(C, n\): Paris-law material constants
  • \(f\): geometry factor
\(a\) vs \(2a\): For a center crack, many texts label total length \(2a\). For an edge crack, length is usually \(a\).
Examples
• Aircraft skin with an inspected crack (damage tolerance)
• Pipeline with detected flaw under pressure cycles
• Bridge weld toe crack under repeated traffic loading
• Structural plate with edge crack under repeated tension cycles
When to use
Cyclic loading with a known crack/flaw that grows with cycles.
