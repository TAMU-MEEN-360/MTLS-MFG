Fatigue (No Flaw) Example

Example 7-10 — Design of a Rotating Shaft

Problem
A solid shaft for a cement kiln produced from the tool steel in Figure 7-18 must be 96 in. long and must survive continuous operation for one year with an applied load of 12,500 lb. The shaft rotates at one revolution per minute during operation.
Design a shaft that will satisfy these requirements.
Step 1 — Determine Required Fatigue Life
The number of loading cycles in one year equals the number of shaft revolutions.
\( N = (1 \; \text{cycle/min})(60 \; \text{min/hr})(24 \; \text{hr/day})(365 \; \text{days/yr}) \)
\( N = 5.256 \times 10^{5} \; \text{cycles} \)
From the S–N curve for this tool steel (Figure 7-18), the allowable stress for this fatigue life is approximately:
\( \sigma_{allow} \approx 72{,}000 \; \text{psi} \)
Step 2 — Determine Shaft Diameter
Using the bending stress relation for a circular shaft:
\( \pm \sigma = \dfrac{32FL}{d^{3}} \)
Substitute the given values:
\( 72{,}000 = \dfrac{32(12{,}500)(96)}{d^{3}} \)
Solving for the diameter:
\( d = 5.54 \; \text{in.} \)
A shaft with a diameter of about 5.54 in would survive one year of operation.
Step 3 — Apply a Factor of Safety
Assume a factor of safety of 2. The allowable stress becomes:
\( \sigma_{design} = \frac{72{,}000}{2} = 36{,}000 \; \text{psi} \)
Substitute this value into the stress equation:
\( 36{,}000 = \dfrac{32(12{,}500)(96)}{d^{3}} \)
Solving for the new required diameter:
\( d = 6.98 \; \text{in.} \)
Recommended shaft diameter: \( d \approx 7.0 \; \text{in.} \)
Engineering Notes
Selecting a larger shaft reduces the stress level and delays fatigue failure. In real cement kiln operation, additional factors may accelerate fatigue, including high temperatures and corrosive environments. Regular inspection of components is therefore essential to prevent catastrophic failure.
